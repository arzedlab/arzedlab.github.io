What Is a SOC Analyst?

Dec 29, 2022 · Ravshan

A SOC analyst is a type of cybersecurity professional who looks mainly at the inner workings of networks. They help prevent security breaches and, when attacks do occur, they work with other people to find out how attackers got in.  Communication is also a big part of being a SOC analyst because they have to be able to explain what they see on their screens and why certain things are happening while they are watching it. This means making sure you know what time zones your local time zone is in, if there’s daylight saving time, etc. An SOC analyst will also help build out proper defenses by finding vulnerabilities in systems and components. This includes identifying threats that could be used to penetrate the system and what steps need to be taken to prevent such attacks from occurring.

The role of an SOC analyst is critical in keeping your business safe from cybercriminals and other malicious actors. It’s important to note that an SOC analyst doesn’t just work in a SOC. They can also be found in other departments, including the security operations center (SOC) itself. An analyst may be working with a team of other analysts and engineers to find vulnerabilities in systems or components such as networks and applications. This helps them identify threats that could be used against your company and what steps need to be taken to prevent such attacks from occurring.

What does someone need to do to become an entry-level SOC analyst?

To become an entry-level SOC analyst, you’ll need at least a bachelor’s degree in computer science or related fields. This is because many of the technical skills associated with being able to analyze systems aren’t something that can be taught in just a few months. Instead, these are developed over time as someone gains experience. Therefore, people who want to enter this field should consider earning their degree first before looking for an opportunity with a company that needs help analyzing threats against their networks and systems.

Are SOC analyst trainings or certifications helpful?

Yes, they are. There are many different certification options available for people who want to become SOC analysts. For example, you can take a course from an accredited institution and receive a certificate of completion that proves you’ve learned the necessary skills to work in this field.

What’s the difference between SOC Tier 1 and Tier 2?

The first thing to understand is that SOCs have different tiers. The top tier is called the SOC, while the second tier is known as a STC or security technology center. In general, the main difference between them is that Tier 1 analysts are responsible for analyzing threats and incidents while Tier 2 analysts deal with the day-to-day operations of their departments.

Conclusion

In conclusion, SOC Analysts generally get on well with people, value teamwork and helping others. They are curious and will like to delve into systems and learn how they work. They are also very independent thinkers who can work on their own, but they will often prefer to be part of a team.
